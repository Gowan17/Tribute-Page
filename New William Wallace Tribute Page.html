Sir William Wallace

The man who fought against tyranny and won his freedom

Picture of Sir William Wallace

  • 1270 - Born in Elderslie,Renfrewshire, Kingdom of Scotland
  • 1297 - Leads a group of 30 men to the scottish town of Lanark, razes the town and kills its English sheriff.
  • 1297 - Fights a larger army than his own at Stirling and wins through superior tactical positioning. He also went on to capture Sitrling Castle.
  • 1297 - Knighted and was appointed guardian of the kingdom.
  • 1298 - Defeated at the Battle of Falkirk by Edward the Longshanks.
  • 1299 - Served as a diplomat to the French and tried to gain their support.
  • 1305 - Captured and arrested by the English near Glasgow.
  • 1305 - Taken to London and condemned as a traitor. Wallace was hanged, disembowled, beheaded and quartered.
  • 1995 - 600 years later the movie 'Braveheart' came out about the life and journey of William Wallace.
